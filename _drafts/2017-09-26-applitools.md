I recently decided to try out Applitools Eyes. Visual regression testing has been getting more and more
popular recently and I think it's a good way to get a lot of value out of testing with a small amount
of up-front investment.

Applitools Eyes is a visual regression testing tool that basically takes screenshots of your site at two
different points in time, and then compares the images. If a difference is detected, the tool lets you
know. At that point, you can mark the diff as a new baseline (an intended change) or a failure 
(an unexpected regression).

# Cons

## Price

Applitools comes with a pretty steep pricetag. They charge $299/month for a single user.
For licenses with more seats, you need to contact them for a quote. I have not done this,
so I can't speak to the cost of these larger plans.

Because the product is so expensive, you may be tempted to buy a single user license and
share the login among multiple users. Unforunately, Applitools recently pushed an update
to "increase security" and "improve accuracy" of the tool. This update (which I assume 
introduces 2FA) will make it impossible to share a single account among several users.

So, just keep in mind that if you decide to use this tool and have multiple employees
that require access, you *must* purchase more than a single license.

## Documentation

I could almost justify the cost, if the documentation weren't so bad.

The documentation is pretty sparse. There are entire sections that are empty and marked as
"under construction." The documentation that does exist is vague and superficial.
